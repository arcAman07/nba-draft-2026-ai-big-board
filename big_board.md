# 2026 NBA Draft, Final Big Board

**Date: June 10, 2026** (draft June 23-24, 2026)

This is the project's final talent-first board, an expected-value ranking of the 2026 class rather than a mock. It is built from five evidence streams: a 6-board market consensus (ESPN, The Ringer, Yahoo/KOC, Tankathon, CBS, Bleacher Report), LODCO-validated machine-learning models trained on the 2000-2021 draft classes (VORP regression, tier classifier, bust logistic), a 781-player historical comps engine producing 15-player statistical cohorts with floor/median/ceiling VORP and bust/All-Star rates, frame-based film study of the top 20 (1 fps stills, honestly labeled, with scouting consensus cited alongside), and 2026 combine measurements and athletic testing. Team fit is deliberately excluded from the ranking; fit lives in the mock draft (data/processed/mock_draft.csv), while this board answers only one question, who returns the most expected value per pick.

## Tier definitions

| Tier | Ranks | Label | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 1-4 | Franchise Cornerstones | Evidence (production, age, cohort) or ceiling strong enough to build around |
| 2 | 5-11 | High-Leverage Starters | Starter-probable profiles, several with live star tails |
| 3 | 12-18 | Starter-or-Bust Swings | Real starter cases with a documented flaw, injury, or data gap on the other side |
| 4 | 19-28 | Rotation Bets with Upside Tails | Likely rotation players whose cohorts carry one famous tail outcome |
| 5 | 29-30 | Specialists / Projects | One elite NBA skill or one defining fit, priced at the end of the round |

# Tier 1 - Franchise Cornerstones (1-4)

## 1. Cameron Boozer - PF, Duke | Age 18.94
- **Measurements:** 6'8.25" barefoot, 252.8 lbs, 7'1.5" wingspan, 9'0" standing reach; modest testing (35.0" max vert, 11.06s lane agility).
- **2025-26:** 22.5/10.2/4.1 with 1.4 spg in 38 GP; class-best 65.3 TS% on 29.9 usage, 39.1% from three, 78.9 FT%; class-best 18.7 BPM with 25.6 AST% vs 12.8 TOV%.
- **Model:** median VORP 19.8 (p25-p75 band 7.0-105.4, the widest and highest in the class); 0.70 All-Star prob; bust prob 0.006, effectively the floor of the dataset. Model rank 1.
- **Consensus 3.0 vs our 1:** model No. 1 by a wide margin, best comp cohort in the class, youngest top prospect with the best efficiency; production + age + cohort outweigh the market's athletic-ceiling preference for Dybantsa.
- **Comps:** floor Grant Williams, ceiling Kevin Love (Bosh and Bogut in the top five); cohort 13.3% bust, 40% All-Star, the best star-hit cohort of the studied eight.
- **Film (ours, stills only):** 685-frame ACC Network reel shows a wide low-post seal into a high-carried rim finish and an already filled-out frame; stills cannot show how quickly he elevates.
- **Best fits:** Memphis at 3 (our mock), Utah at 2, Washington at 1.
- **Verdict:** The rare No. 1 case built on evidence rather than projection, best BPM, best TS%-at-usage, youngest top prospect, near-empty downside tail. The honest concern is athletic ceiling, which is why the market slots him third; this board takes elite production over the more aesthetic bet.

## 2. AJ Dybantsa - SF, BYU | Age 19.40

- **Model:** median VORP 3.6 (band 1.2-27.9), 0.49 All-Star prob (second-best in class), bust prob 0.053. Model rank 8, dinged for efficiency relative to load.

- **Comps:** floor Jarrett Culver, ceiling Mike Miller; cohort 0% bust but only 20% All-Star, milder than the scouting hype implies (two size-mismatch flags).
- **Film (ours, stills only):** 1,503-frame Big 12 reel shows a high-apex release well above contests and long balanced rim extension; 1 fps sampling cannot show burst or first-step speed.
- **Best fits:** Washington at 1 (our mock), Utah at 2, Memphis at 3 as the backstop.
- **Verdict:** The class's clearest bet on scoring talent, 25.5 a game as a teen wing with a 42-inch vert. The model's skepticism is legible (efficiency did not keep pace with enormous usage), so he stays Tier 1 at No. 2, behind Boozer on evidence and ahead of everyone else on ceiling.
