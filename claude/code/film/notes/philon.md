## Frame-based observations (mine)

- Drive and finish at North Carolina (frame_0152.jpg through frame_0155.jpg). He receives in transition on the left wing with his body already low (frame_0152), splits two UNC defenders at the foul line off a low crossover with the ball kept tight to his hip (frame_0153), gathers through traffic at the restricted area (frame_0154), and the close-up in frame_0155 shows him extending fully with his right arm to finish high off the glass over UNC's #3 and #11. Best finishing-extension evidence in the sample; the frames cannot show how much speed created the gap.
- Lane finish vs Arkansas State (frame_0186.jpg through frame_0189.jpg). He drives left into the lane with a deep knee bend and the ball dribbled low and outside his frame against the defender (frame_0187), elevates in the paint with the ball raised (frame_0188), and frame_0189 shows the Alabama bench with arms up as the play finishes. Supports a low, controlled handle posture into a finish; touch at the rim falls between samples.
- Handle and survey posture vs Oregon (frame_0102.jpg through frame_0104.jpg). Bringing the ball up and sizing up at the left wing he stays in a bent-knee stance with the ball kept on his outside hand, chest up and eyes on the defense across consecutive seconds. Frames support posture and ball placement only, not change-of-pace or shake.
- Body frame in game context (frame_0005.jpg, frame_0155.jpg). The title-card image and the UNC close-up both show a lean, narrow-shouldered guard build with long arms for 6'4, visibly skinnier through the chest and legs than the college wings around him. Frames cannot establish strength.
- On-screen recruiting context (frame_0205.jpg). An SEC Network graphic during the UNC Asheville game lists Alabama's freshman additions by ESPN Top 100 rank, with L. Philon at 32nd. Frame-visible broadcast graphic, not my evaluation.
- Shooting mechanics caveat. The sampled frames did not capture a clean catch-and-shoot or pull-up jumper rep for him (the reel skews to drives and finishes), so I make no frame-based claim about his set point, base width, or follow-through. The title card's 28% 3P through 11 games is the only shooting evidence in the frames.
- Not claimed from frames, burst, pace manipulation, passing feel, defensive timing. Highlight reel bias toward made plays applies.

## Sourced scouting observations (human scouts, cited)

- Babcock Hoops calls Philon's handle "on-a-string" and probably best-in-class, strong driving with either hand using snake dribbles, wide crossovers, and step-backs to break down defenses, with three-level scoring upside and high-level pick-and-roll play. Source, Babcock Hoops, https://www.babcockhoops.com/post/2026-nba-draft-labaron-philon-scouting-report
- Yahoo Sports' mock-draft scouting report says returning to Alabama was the right bet, with Philon roughly doubling his scoring as a sophomore (about 21 points per game on 51 percent shooting, around 5 assists) and improving from 31.5 percent to roughly 39.7 percent from three, while noting he is somewhat skinny and must add muscle, and that his size and twitch likely cap him below plus defender status in the NBA. Source, Yahoo Sports, https://sports.yahoo.com/articles/labaron-philon-jr-nba-mock-212151698.html
- Following the combine, ESPN's mock draft projected Philon at No. 13 overall to the Miami Heat, per Roundtable's coverage of his NBA draft green-room invite. Source, Roundtable, https://roundtable.io/sports/ncaa/alabama/news/alabama-s-labaron-philon-invited-to-attend-2026-nba-draft
