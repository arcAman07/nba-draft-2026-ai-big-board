# Karim Lopez (SF/PF, New Zealand Breakers, NBL) - Film Notes, 2026 NBA Draft
Source clip, NBL Round 19 vs Melbourne United, 32-point game (YouTube id _Ub3ojrAnsA), 200 frames at 1 fps. Lopez wears #1 in the dark Breakers jersey.

## Frame-based observations (mine)
- Finishing extension on the move. He elevates off one foot and fully extends his right arm for a finger-roll style finish above the rim line, ball balanced one-handed at full reach with shoulders still mostly square. Length at the release point is obvious against NBL pros. (frame_0163.jpg, frame_0164.jpg, frame_0165.jpg, frame_0166.jpg)
- Downhill handle posture. Attacking in the open floor he pushes the ball ahead with his right hand, torso leaning into the drive, ball kept outside his frame away from the help defender rather than high and exposed. (frame_0163.jpg, frame_0164.jpg)
- Paint touch sequence late in the game. With the score 90-92 he works from the left wing into the lane and gets a shot up at the rim through two defenders over a four-second sequence; he keeps his dribble alive into the paint rather than settling. (frame_0172.jpg, frame_0173.jpg, frame_0174.jpg, frame_0175.jpg)
- Body frame in game context. Close-ups show a broad-shouldered but still wiry upper body, more developed through the shoulders than the trunk; he looks like a wing carrying real but not maxed-out muscle. (frame_0146.jpg, frame_0190.jpg)
- Role and floor positioning. In the fourth quarter he is repeatedly the Breaker initiating from the wing or top rather than spotting up in the corner; the scoreboard graphic at 4:16 credits him with 21 points on 8/9 FG at that point. (frame_0138.jpg, frame_0139.jpg, frame_0172.jpg)
- Frames cannot support claims about his burst, shot speed, or defensive reaction time, so none are made here.

## Sourced scouting observations (human scouts, cited)
- Wilko Martínez-Cachero, Floor and Ceiling (Substack): his three-point shot "does not look busted but his volume and percentages need to tick up"; it is "not overly quick" and he "struggles to convert open spot-ups with regularity" (32.2% from three). Defensively he "loses focus away from the ball too often" and is "flat-footed on the perimeter." https://floorandceiling.substack.com/p/karim-lopez-scouting-report
- Floor and Ceiling, same report: "strong positional size for an NBA wing at about 6-foot-9, 225 pounds with a 7-foot-1 wingspan"; as a scorer he "loves to drive and get into the paint," though his "handle and balance need to keep improving." https://floorandceiling.substack.com/p/karim-lopez-scouting-report
- Babcock Hoops scouting report: "a long, athletic forward who plays with poise and natural feel," strong around the basket "embracing contact and converting through physicality," and he "uses length to guard multiple positions" with advanced timing for his age. https://www.babcockhoops.com/post/2026-nba-draft-karim-lopez-scouting-report
