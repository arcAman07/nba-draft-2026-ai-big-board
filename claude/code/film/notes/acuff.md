# Darius Acuff Jr. (PG, Arkansas) - Film Notes
Clip studied: acuff_HJmg3oa_gMI.webm, "Career-High 49 Pts vs Alabama, 2026.02.18" (ESPN broadcast, ~5:16, 316 frames at 1 fps)

## Frame-based observations (mine)

- Shooting gather/set point: on the baseline pull-up sequence (frame_0145.jpg, frame_0146.jpg, frame_0147.jpg, frame_0148.jpg) Acuff brings the ball up through the center of his chest to a set point at roughly forehead height before release. In frame_0147.jpg the ball sits just above eye level with elbow tucked under it and feet roughly shoulder width, and frame_0148.jpg shows the resulting shot with high arc, the ball peaking above the top of the backboard, consistent with a release that gets good elevation on the arc even with a contesting defender nearby in 0147.
- Second shot sequence: frames frame_0108.jpg, frame_0109.jpg, frame_0110.jpg, frame_0111.jpg, frame_0112.jpg capture a possession ending in a jumper. In frame_0110.jpg he is gathered with the ball brought to the chest in traffic near the wing; frame_0112.jpg shows the ball in flight on a high trajectory. Motion blur in 0111 limits what can be said about the exact release frame.
- Handle posture: frame_0223.jpg is a clean close-up of his isolation stance, knees bent, hips dropped below the defender's level, ball on a low between-the-legs dribble at calf height, eyes up scanning two defenders. The low dribble height and flat back are visible and repeatable across frame_0135.jpg, where he attacks Alabama's #5 (Allen) with a low crossover, shoulders dipped below the defender's chest.
- Body frame/build: in the close-ups (frame_0135.jpg, frame_0223.jpg, frame_0147.jpg) he reads as a lean guard, slim through the shoulders and arms with thin legs, more wiry than bulky. He absorbs contact on the drive in frame_0145.jpg and frame_0146.jpg while keeping the ball protected on his hip, but his torso visibly bends away from the contact, suggesting he plays through contact with angles rather than mass.
- Finishing/drive posture: frame_0145.jpg and frame_0146.jpg show a baseline drive into two defenders where he keeps a low center of gravity and shields the ball on the far hip. Frames do not show enough of the rim finish itself to assess extension at the basket.
- Frames cannot support claims about his burst, separation speed, or decision timing; those require continuous video.

## Sourced scouting observations (human scouts, cited)

- Floor & Ceiling (substack scouting report) notes Acuff "put together truly one of the best scoring seasons ever seen for a true freshman guard, scoring 23.5 points on 48% shooting... at all three levels" and credits him with "likely the best floater in the 2026 draft class" (30 of 65, ~46%). Source, https://floorandceiling.substack.com/p/darius-acuff-scouting-report
- Babcock Hoops scouting report describes him as "a confident, physical, score-first guard who can tilt a game with shot-making, downhill pressure, and pick-and-roll creation," while flagging defense as the swing skill. Source, https://www.babcockhoops.com/post/2026-nba-draft-darius-acuff-jr-scouting-report
- Sactown Sports scouting piece raises the defensive concerns directly, paraphrased, he "dies on screens, gets beat off the dribble too easily" and does not consistently play with enough effort on that end, and notes he is a below-the-rim finisher (5-7 dunks on the season, 36.5-inch vertical). Source, https://sactownsports.com/nba/sactown-scouting-darius-acuff
- Bleacher Report (Jonathan Wasserman) mocked Acuff at No. 5 after he declared, with some scouts viewing him as the top point guard in the class. Source, https://bleacherreport.com/articles/25421398-arkansas-darius-acuff-jr-declares-2026-nba-draft-latest-br-mock-draft-projection
