## Source cross-check and discrepancies

Sources compared: Tankathon full-draft page, CBS Sports draft-order article, ESPN lottery-results article, Wikipedia 2026 NBA draft page.

- Teams selecting at all 30 first-round slots: Tankathon and CBS Sports agree exactly, pick for pick. ESPN (picks 1-14) and Wikipedia (1-30) also agree.
- Pick 12 notation discrepancy: Tankathon, ESPN, and Wikipedia show pick 12 as Oklahoma City "via LA Clippers" (from the July 2019 SGA/Paul George trade). The CBS Sports list showed pick 12 as Oklahoma City Thunder with no "via" note. Majority of sources (3 of 4) support "via LA Clippers"; recorded as such in the CSV.
- Wikipedia fetch artifact: a summary section returned from the Wikipedia fetch stated "3rd: Brooklyn Nets," contradicting the pick-by-pick list on the same page (Memphis 3rd, Brooklyn 6th). ESPN, CBS, and Tankathon all confirm Memphis at 3 and Brooklyn at 6, so the pick list is treated as authoritative and the summary line as an extraction error.
- Pick 16 routing: Tankathon/CBS show simply "via Phoenix"; Wikipedia shows the full chain "from Phoenix via Orlando" (Memphis acquired it from Orlando in the June 2025 KCP/Cole Anthony trade). No conflict, just differing detail levels.
- Pick 18 routing: Tankathon/CBS show "via Orlando"; Wikipedia shows "from Orlando via Phoenix" (Charlotte acquired it from Phoenix in the Feb 2025 Nurkic trade). No conflict.
- Pick 30 routing: Wikipedia describes a multi-step chain (OKC pick to Philadelphia in a 2023 trade, then to Washington in 2025, then to Dallas in a 2026 three-team Anthony Davis trade). The exact intermediate trade dates/terms in that chain were only obtained from a single source (Wikipedia) and the full player details of each leg are UNVERIFIED beyond that page; the end state (Dallas selecting at 30, pick originally Oklahoma City's) is confirmed by Tankathon and CBS.
- Pick 22 routing: original team Houston confirmed by Tankathon/CBS ("via Houston"); the intermediate step (HOU pick held by OKC since the July 2019 trade, sent to Philadelphia in Feb 2026) comes from Wikipedia only, so the intermediate details are single-source.
- NBA.com's official draft-order article (nba.com/news/2026-nba-draft-order) timed out on two fetch attempts and could not be read directly; it is listed in sources as attempted. Verification therefore rests on ESPN, CBS Sports, Tankathon, and Wikipedia, which are fully consistent on teams at every pick.
