## Pick 1: Washington Wizards
- Picks held: No. 1 overall (won the lottery with 14% odds, first No. 1 since John Wall in 2010).
- Core and timeline: Rebuilding, but vet-accelerated. Young core of Alex Sarr (16.3 ppg, 2.0 bpg, second in NBA in blocks behind Wembanyama), Bilal Coulibaly, Tre Johnson, Bub Carrington, Kyshawn George.
- Major change: Acquired Trae Young from Atlanta in January (for CJ McCollum and Corey Kispert) and Anthony Davis from Dallas at the February deadline (with D'Angelo Russell, Jaden Hardy, Dante Exum).
- 2025-26 in one line: League-worst 17-65; Young played only 5 games (back/quad) and Davis never suited up, so the tank rolled on.
- Needs: 1) Best player available franchise talent at No. 1 (Dybantsa/Boozer/Peterson tier), 2) wing shooting around Sarr and Young, 3) defensive identity on the perimeter.
- Cap: Took on roughly $100M of Young plus Davis salary, so flexibility is spent, but no luxury-tax pressure on a 17-win roster.
- Draft tendencies (2023-25): Swing on upside teens almost exclusively, Coulibaly 18, Sarr and Carrington 19, Tre Johnson 19.

## Pick 2: Utah Jazz
- Picks held: No. 2 overall (jumped from a tied-for-worst 22-60 record).
- Core and timeline: Rebuilding but trying to turn the corner. Lauri Markkanen (26.7 ppg when healthy, injury-shortened year), Jaren Jackson Jr. acquired from Memphis at the deadline, Keyonte George (team-leading scorer), Walker Kessler (season-ending injury), Ace Bailey, Brice Sensabaugh, Cody Williams.
- 2025-26 in one line: 22-60 as expected, but the post-deadline Markkanen/JJJ frontcourt flashed before injuries shut everyone down (Kessler, JJJ, Nurkic all out by spring).
- Needs: 1) Lead initiator/No. 1 perimeter option, 2) point-of-attack defense, 3) center depth behind the injured Kessler.
- Cap: Comfortably under the tax with flexibility; JJJ's next contract is the big line item to manage.
- Draft tendencies (2023-25): Volume swings on upside teens (Hendricks, Cody Williams, Ace Bailey); also traded for older guard Walter Clayton Jr. in 2025, then moved him to Memphis in the JJJ deal.

## Pick 3 (and 16): Memphis Grizzlies
- Picks held: No. 3 (own) and No. 16 (via Phoenix). One entry covers both.
- Core and timeline: Full rebuild. Traded Jaren Jackson Jr. to Utah at the deadline for Taylor Hendricks, Walter Clayton Jr., Kyle Anderson, Georges Niang and three first-round picks; ESPN reports the team is ready to move Ja Morant (2 years, ~$87M left) this offseason after a lukewarm deadline market.
- Remaining young pieces: Jaylen Wells, Zach Edey, GG Jackson, Cedric Coward, Hendricks, Clayton.
- 2025-26 in one line: 25-57 collapse that ended the Morant-era core, with both JJJ and Desmond Bane long gone.
- Needs: 1) A franchise wing/scorer at 3, 2) lead guard succession if/when Morant is dealt, 3) shooting everywhere.
- Cap: Under the tax and shedding long money; Morant's deal is the last big contract to resolve.
- Draft tendencies (2023-25): Older, productive plug-and-play college players (Edey, Coward, Wells), and they trade up when they like someone.

## Pick 4 (and 15): Chicago Bulls
- Picks held: No. 4 (own, leapt up despite just 3% top-4 odds) and No. 15 (via Portland).
- Core and timeline: Rebuilding/retooling around a young pair. Josh Giddey (17.0/8.3/9.1 in 54 games, offseason ankle scope, ready for camp) and Matas Buzelis (16.3 ppg, 1.5 bpg, 77 starts); added Collin Sexton and Ousmane Dieng at the deadline when Coby White went to Charlotte; Noa Essengue developing.
- 2025-26 in one line: 31-51, season effectively over by March, but lottery night was a franchise-altering win.
- Needs: 1) Rim-protecting center, 2) two-way wings, 3) shooting to space Giddey drives.
- Cap: Projected as one of the league's few cap-space teams this summer (per Spotrac).
- Draft tendencies (2023-25): Teen upside swings (Buzelis, Essengue), light on draft capital before this cycle.

## Pick 5: LA Clippers (via Indiana)
- Picks held: No. 5 (via Indiana, from the deadline Zubac trade; Indiana's pick conveyed because it landed 5-9). Own 2026 pick (No. 12) goes to OKC.
- Core and timeline: Aging retool on the fly. Traded James Harden to Cleveland for Darius Garland at the deadline; sent Ivica Zubac to Indiana for Bennedict Mathurin, Isaiah Jackson, this pick and an unprotected 2029 first. Kawhi Leonard (turns 35 in 2026) anchors with Garland and Mathurin.
- 2025-26 in one line: 42-40, 9th in the West, eliminated in the play-in, but won lottery night when Indiana's pick landed at exactly No. 5.
- Needs: 1) Starting center (Zubac's departure left Isaiah Jackson), 2) youth and athleticism on the wing, 3) long-term star insurance behind Kawhi.
- Cap: Over the cap with big vet money (Kawhi, Garland); no realistic cap room, mid-tier tax pressure. UNVERIFIED exact tax position.
- Draft tendencies (2023-25): Older win-now picks at the back of round one (Kobe Brown, Niederhauser); rarely this high; this is their best pick in decades.

## Pick 6: Brooklyn Nets
- Picks held: No. 6 overall.
- Core and timeline: Year-one full rebuild. The historic five-first-rounder 2025 class (Egor Demin, Nolan Traore, Drake Powell, Ben Saraf, Danny Wolf) plus Cam Thomas (re-signed Sept 2025), Michael Porter Jr., Nic Claxton, Noah Clowney.
- 2025-26 in one line: 20-62, exactly the development-and-lottery season the front office planned, though they fell from tied-best odds to No. 6.
- Needs: 1) Best available cornerstone talent, 2) defensive identity, 3) wing size; they can take BPA at 6 regardless of fit.
- Cap: Most projected cap space in the NBA (~$48-50M), positioned as the league's salary clearing house.
- Draft tendencies (2023-25): Hoard picks, lean teen international/upside (Demin, Traore, Saraf); media has floated the No. 6 pick in Ja Morant frameworks.

## Pick 7: Sacramento Kings
- Picks held: No. 7 overall.
- Core and timeline: Full teardown declared under new front office boss Scott Perry. Domantas Sabonis trade talks (notably with Toronto) were tabled until this offseason; Zach LaVine (season-ending hand surgery, deal expires after 2026-27) and DeMar DeRozan remain hard to move. Young pieces: Devin Carter, Nique Clifford, Keon Ellis, Maxime Raynaud.
- 2025-26 in one line: 22-60, bottom of the West, the season that formally ended the Sabonis-LaVine-DeRozan experiment.
- Needs: 1) Franchise lead guard, 2) wings of any kind, 3) draft capital and young talent everywhere.
- Cap: Over the cap with unwanted vet money (Sabonis ~$42M and ~$94M over two more years); shedding salary is the offseason job.
- Draft tendencies (2023-25): Older plug-and-play college players (Carter 22, Clifford 23, Raynaud), not teen projects.

## Pick 8 (and 23): Atlanta Hawks
- Picks held: No. 8 (via New Orleans) and No. 23 (via Cleveland). Own pick (No. 20) goes to San Antonio.
- Core and timeline: Young playoff team that chose a clean break. Traded Trae Young to Washington in January (got CJ McCollum's expiring deal and Corey Kispert back) and flipped Kristaps Porzingis to Golden State for Jonathan Kuminga and Buddy Hield at the deadline. Core: Jalen Johnson, Dyson Daniels, Zaccharie Risacher, Onyeka Okongwu, Kuminga, Asa Newell.
- 2025-26 in one line: 46-36, 6 seed even after dealing Trae, then a first-round exit (Knicks side of the bracket).
- Needs: 1) Lead guard/half-court creation post-Trae, 2) shooting, 3) center depth after Porzingis left.
- Cap: Real flexibility with McCollum's expiring money coming off; no tax pressure. UNVERIFIED exact room.
- Draft tendencies (2023-25): Young upside (Risacher No. 1, Bufkin, Newell) and aggressive pick accumulation (the unprotected NOP pick at 8 is the prize of an earlier trade).

## Pick 9 (and 30): Dallas Mavericks
- Picks held: No. 9 (own) and No. 30 (via Oklahoma City).
- Core and timeline: Re-centered on Cooper Flagg. Ended the Anthony Davis experiment at the deadline, sending him to Washington in a multi-team deal for Khris Middleton, AJ Johnson, Tyus Jones, Marvin Bagley III and picks. Kyrie Irving missed the entire season (ACL) but returns for 2026-27.
- 2025-26 in one line: 26-56 team result, but Flagg's rookie year was a triumph, 20.2/6.6/4.5 and team leader in points, rebounds, assists and steals.
- Needs: 1) Secondary shot creator next to Flagg and Kyrie, 2) wing shooting, 3) athletic depth; No. 30 suits a ready role player.
- Cap: Shed Davis's long-term max in the deadline deal; books are meaningfully cleaner going forward. UNVERIFIED exact room.
- Draft tendencies (2023-25): Swing big when they pick (Lively, Flagg); traded most other capital during the win-now era.

## Pick 10: Milwaukee Bucks
- Picks held: No. 10 overall.
- Core and timeline: At the fork. Giannis Antetokounmpo (27.6/9.8/5.4 in only 36 games) reportedly told the Bucks to trade him multiple times during the season; the front office is now "open for business" and ownership wants direction set before draft night. Miami is the most aggressive suitor and Giannis prefers to stay East. Supporting cast: Myles Turner, Kevin Porter Jr., Kyle Kuzma. UNVERIFIED full depth chart.
- 2025-26 in one line: A disastrous 32-50 that ended an era of contention and put the franchise icon on the block.
- Needs: Entirely contingent on the Giannis decision; broadly 1) young guard creation, 2) athletic wings, 3) restocked draft capital.
- Cap: Over the cap with Giannis's supermax dominating the sheet; a trade resets everything.
- Draft tendencies (2023-25): Rarely kept firsts (traded most), took teen upside when they did (AJ Johnson 2024).

## Pick 11: Golden State Warriors
- Picks held: No. 11 overall.
- Core and timeline: End-of-era contender trying one more push. Curry (38), Jimmy Butler (36) and Draymond (36) remain; traded Jonathan Kuminga and Buddy Hield to Atlanta for Kristaps Porzingis at the deadline; named in Giannis destination reporting.
- 2025-26 in one line: 37-45, 10th in the West, eliminated in the play-in, the first clearly bad year of the late-Curry window.
- Needs: 1) Youth and athleticism on the wing, 2) long-term center if Porzingis (expiring) walks, 3) on-ball creation behind Curry.
- Cap: Expensive vet roster, ongoing tax pressure; Porzingis coming off the books is the main relief valve. UNVERIFIED exact apron status.
- Draft tendencies (2023-25): Older plug-and-play college players (Podziemski, Trayce Jackson-Davis, Quinten Post, Will Richard).

## Pick 12 (and 17): Oklahoma City Thunder
- Picks held: No. 12 (via LA Clippers) and No. 17 (via Philadelphia, the long-awaited protected pick finally conveying). One entry covers both.
- Core and timeline: Prime contender. Defending 2025 champions led by SGA, Chet Holmgren, Jalen Williams, Cason Wallace, Thomas Sorber.
- 2025-26 in one line: League-best 64-18 and a 7-0 playoff start before falling to San Antonio in a seven-game West finals classic.
- Needs: 1) Cheap rookie-scale depth as extensions kick in, 2) backup center, 3) shooting; prime draft-and-stash or trade-down candidates with two mid-firsts.
- Cap: The SGA/Chet/J-Will extensions begin landing in 2026-27, pushing a homegrown core into tax/apron territory, which makes these picks extra valuable.
- Draft tendencies (2023-25): The league's model drafting org, productive college players plus patience (Wallace, Topic stash year, Sorber), and a still-enormous pick warchest.

## Pick 13: Miami Heat
- Picks held: No. 13 overall.
- Core and timeline: Star-hunting retool. Bam Adebayo is the only untouchable as Miami aggressively pursues Giannis; Tyler Herro, Norman Powell, Kel'el Ware, Jaime Jaquez Jr., Kasparas Jakucionis fill out the core.
- 2025-26 in one line: 43-39, 10th in the East, season ended with an overtime play-in loss to Charlotte.
- Needs: 1) A true No. 1 star (hence the Giannis push), 2) lead guard playmaking, 3) wing defense.
- Cap: Capped out with mid-tier salaries that exist mostly as trade ballast for a star deal. UNVERIFIED exact tax position.
- Draft tendencies (2023-25): Mixed, older college producers (Jaquez) and a teen international swing (Jakucionis); develop second-rounders and undrafted players as well as anyone.

## Pick 14 (and 18): Charlotte Hornets
- Picks held: No. 14 (own) and No. 18 (via Orlando). One entry covers both.
- Core and timeline: Ascending young team. LaMelo Ball (20.1 ppg; he and rookie Kon Knueppel became the second teammate pair ever with 260+ threes each, joining Curry/Klay), Brandon Miller, Knueppel, plus Coby White acquired from Chicago at the deadline (Sexton out).
- 2025-26 in one line: 44-38, the best season of the LaMelo era, beat Miami in the play-in before losing the 8-seed game to Orlando.
- Needs: 1) Starting-caliber center, 2) wing/point-of-attack defense, 3) bench depth to survive LaMelo absences.
- Cap: Relatively clean books with no tax pressure as the young core remains on rookie-scale deals. UNVERIFIED exact room.
- Draft tendencies (2023-25): Shifted from teen projects (Salaun 2024) to ready-made skill (Knueppel, McNeeley, Sion James, Kalkbrenner in 2025).

## Pick 20: San Antonio Spurs (via Atlanta)
- Picks held: No. 20 (via Atlanta). Own pick (No. 29) goes to Cleveland.
- Core and timeline: Arrived ahead of schedule, contending now. Victor Wembanyama, De'Aaron Fox, Stephon Castle, Dylan Harper, Devin Vassell, Carter Bryant.
- 2025-26 in one line: 62-20, beat the defending-champion Thunder in a seven-game West finals, and trail the Knicks 2-1 in an ongoing NBA Finals.
- Needs: 1) Veteran shooting, 2) wing depth for the playoff rotation, 3) frontcourt insurance behind Wembanyama.
- Cap: Fox max on the books and a Wembanyama extension looming (eligible this summer, would start 2027-28); cheap rookie deals still carry the depth, tax pressure coming, not here yet.
- Draft tendencies (2023-25): Upside teens at the top (Wemby, Castle, Harper, Bryant) plus comfort with stash picks.

## Pick 21: Detroit Pistons (via Minnesota)
- Picks held: No. 21 (via Minnesota). Own pick (No. 28) goes to Minnesota (pick-swap outcome of a prior trade).
- Core and timeline: Young contender. Cade Cunningham as an MVP-ballot engine with Jalen Duren, Ausar Thompson, Ron Holland, Jaden Ivey.
- 2025-26 in one line: A franchise-record-pace 60-22 for the East's No. 1 seed, survived Orlando in seven, then lost a Game 7 heartbreaker to Cleveland in the semis.

- Cap: Could open $30-40M only by renouncing/declining options (per Spotrac); more likely operate over the cap and retain their own.
- Draft tendencies (2023-25): Teen upside swings in the rebuild years (Ausar, Holland), no 2025 first; now drafting for a winner.

## Pick 24: New York Knicks

- Core and timeline: Peak contention, playing in the Finals right now. Jalen Brunson, Karl-Anthony Towns, Mikal Bridges, OG Anunoby, Josh Hart, Mitchell Robinson.
- 2025-26 in one line: 53-29 as the East 3 seed, swept Philadelphia and Cleveland in succession, and lead San Antonio 2-1 in the NBA Finals as of June 10.

- Cap: Named by Spotrac as a potential cap-space team this summer, entirely contingent on LeBron's decision.
- Draft tendencies (2023-25): Older plug-and-play picks (Knecht), little capital retained.

## Pick 26: Denver Nuggets
- Picks held: No. 26 overall.
- Core and timeline: Contending but stressed window. Nikola Jokic, Jamal Murray, Aaron Gordon, Christian Braun, Cam Johnson (acquired for MPJ in 2025).
- 2025-26 in one line: 54-28 was good for the 3 seed, then a 4-2 first-round upset loss to Minnesota, their earliest exit of the Jokic prime.

## Pick 27: Boston Celtics

- Core and timeline: Re-contending in 2026-27. Jayson Tatum returned in March, under 10 months after his Achilles tear and ahead of schedule; Jaylen Brown, Derrick White, Payton Pritchard remain, and Nikola Vucevic was added at the deadline.
