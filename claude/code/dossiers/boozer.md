# 1. Cameron Boozer — PF, Duke (Tier 1)

**Age at draft:** 18.94 | **Measurements:** 6'8.25" without shoes (height in shoes did not measure), 252.8 lbs, 7'1.5" wingspan (85.5"), 9'0" standing reach (108").
Athletic testing was modest by lottery standards, 28.5" standing vert, 35.0" max vert, 11.06s lane agility, 2.99s shuttle, 3.31s sprint.
Hand measurements and body fat blank (did not measure). Skipped 5-on-5 scrimmages per ESPN.

## Production (2025-26)

22.5 ppg, 10.2 rpg, 4.1 apg, 1.4 spg, 0.6 bpg in 33.4 mpg across all 38 games, no significant injuries found.
The efficiency is the headline, 55.6 FG%, 39.1% from three on 3.6 attempts, 78.9 FT% (7.4 FTA/g), and a class-best 65.3 TS% on a 29.9 usage rate.
Advanced profile is enormous, 25.6 AST% against 12.8 TOV%.
His 18.7 BPM (12.7 offensive, 6.0 defensive) is the best in this prospect group by a wide margin.

## Model view

Predicted VORP median of 19.8 with a p25-p75 band of 7.0 to 105.4, the widest and highest distribution in the class (mean 22.4).
Tier probabilities, 0.70 All-Star and 0.20 Starter, with a bust probability of 0.006, effectively the floor of the dataset. No flags.
The model loves him because he hits every one of its strongest features at once, the youngest age among top prospects (18.94), the class-best college BPM (18.7), elite TS% (65.3) and an unusually high AST% (25.6) for a big.

## Market view & board delta

Consensus median 3.0, with a min of 1 (Tankathon) and a max of 3 (spread 2), so the market sees him as a clear top-three player but mostly third. I have him 1st.
The model ranks him No. 1 by a wide margin with a 0.70 All-Star probability, and his comp cohort (Love, Bosh, Bogut) carries a 40% All-Star rate, the best in the class.
He is also the youngest top prospect at 18.94 with the best efficiency line in the group, 65.3 TS% on 29.9 usage.
The market's preference for Dybantsa's athletic ceiling is real, but on production, age, and cohort evidence Boozer is the pick.

## Historical comps & outcomes

Floor comp Grant Williams (2019, pick 22, 1.0 career VORP); ceiling comp Kevin Love (2008, pick 5, 36.7 VORP), with Chris Bosh (31.1) and Andrew Bogut (16.0) also in the top five.
The 15-player cohort runs from a 0.9 floor VORP through a 12.8 median to a 67.4 ceiling, with a 13.3% bust rate and a 40% All-Star rate, comfortably the best star-hit cohort of these eight prospects.
Read probabilistically, roughly two in five players who looked like Boozer became All-Stars and almost none washed out.
One flagged mismatch, comp Ryan Anderson is a creation-role mismatch (Boozer's 25.6 AST% vs Anderson's 9.7), meaning Boozer brings playmaking those comps lacked. Confidence HIGH.

## Verdict

Boozer is the rare No. 1 case built on evidence rather than projection.
He posted the class's best BPM, best TS%-at-usage combination, and best rebounding line while being the youngest top prospect, and the model responds with a 0.70 All-Star probability and a near-zero bust number.
His comp cohort (Love, Bosh, Bogut) hit at 40%, and his playmaking exceeds those comps.
The honest concern is athletic ceiling, the combine verticals were ordinary and scouts agree he is not an explosive athlete, which is why the market slots him third.
I will take the player with elite production, elite youth, and a near-empty downside tail over the more aesthetic bet.
