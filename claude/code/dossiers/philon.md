## Verdict

Philon's sophomore season is exactly what you ask a fringe-lottery guard to do: 22 points and 5 assists at 62.6 TS% on 30% usage, with the three-ball jumping to 39.9% on real volume.
The model puts him 10th with a fat upside tail, the cohort carries a 20% All-Star rate, and Lillard sits in the comp list as the reminder of what small score-first guards occasionally become.
The discounts are honest ones: a 176-pound frame, scouts who doubt the defensive ceiling, and the fact that my own frame study only covers his freshman reel with no jumper rep sampled.
At 13 he is solid two-way guard value with a live star tail, and the right buyer is a team that can let the frame catch up to the skill.
