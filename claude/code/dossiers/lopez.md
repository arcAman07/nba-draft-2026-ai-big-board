## Verdict

Lopez is the class's purest uncertainty trade: a 19.2-year-old who held a real rotation role and scored efficiently inside the arc against NBL professionals, attached to a data file where every advanced number is unverified and every comp is stamped LOW confidence.
The shape of the evidence is good, Deng, Miller and Wagner in the statistical neighborhood, a 20% nominal cohort All-Star rate, poised driving and physical finishing on film, while the resolution of the evidence is poor, which is the entire reason he sits 16th rather than 10th.
The 16-spot spread across public boards says the league's evaluators are as split as our sources, and the scouted flaws (slow, low-volume three, perimeter focus lapses) are the kind that decide whether the Deng tail or the Chriss floor shows up.
He belongs in the 10-16 range on optionality alone, and at 16 the variance is finally priced in my favor.
