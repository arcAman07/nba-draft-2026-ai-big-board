# 14. Mikel Brown Jr. — PG, Louisville (Tier 3)

**Age at draft:** 20.23 | **Measurements:** 6'3.5" barefoot (75.5"), 190.2 lbs, 6'7.5" wingspan (79.5"), 8'4.5" standing reach (100.5"); standout athletic tests: 10.57s lane agility (fastest among my eight prospects), 33.5" standing vertical, 39.5" max vertical, 2.89s shuttle, 3.24s sprint.

## Production (2025-26)

- 18.2 ppg, 3.3 rpg, 4.7 apg, 1.2 spg, 0.1 bpg in 29.2 mpg, but across only 21 games: a lower back injury cost him 14 games total, an 8-game absence from Dec 13 to Jan 23 and then the final 6 including the ACC and NCAA tournaments.
- Volume without efficiency: 41.0 FG%, 34.4 3P% on a heavy 7.6 attempts per game, 57.7 TS% on 31.4% usage; the 84.4 FT% is the one genuinely strong shooting indicator.
- Creation came with leaks: 30.3 AST% against a 16.4 TOV%, the worst turnover rate in the top 20.
- Advanced: 6.6 BPM (4.9 OBPM, 1.7 DBPM), by far the weakest of any prospect ranked 9-16 on this board.
- Every per-game number needs the 21-game asterisk in both directions: small sample, but also a sample that includes zero postseason basketball.

## Market view & board delta

Consensus median 7.5 (min 5, max 9, spread 4); my final rank is 14, making him the biggest faller on my board, six and a half spots below market, while the model would drop him to 28.
Board rationale: "The board's biggest faller: 0.33 bust prob (worst in the top 20), 16.4 TOV%, 6.6 BPM, back injury limited him to 21 GP; market is buying pedigree and passing flair, the production evidence does not support top-8; D'Angelo Russell/Klay upside keeps him at 14 rather than lower."
Expanded: every outlet has him between 5 and 9 because the eye-test inputs are real, clean mechanics, the best pick-and-roll passing flashes in the guard class, and a pre-season top-10 pedigree; none of that showed up in the production, which at 41% from the field with the class's worst turnover economy reads more like a warning than a lottery case.
The honest counterweight is that 21 games around a back injury is a censored sample and his comp cohort never busted, so 14 is the compromise: well below the market's 7.5 because the evidence is bad, well above the model's 28 because the evidence is also thin.

## Verdict

Brown is the clearest market-versus-evidence collision in this class, and my 14 is a deliberate vote against the market's 7.5.
What scouts see is real, clean mechanics, the best passing flashes in the guard group, elite agility testing, but the season he actually played produced a 6.6 BPM, 41% shooting and the worst turnover rate in the top 20, and the bust model prices that at one in three.
His 0%-bust, 26.7%-All-Star comp cohort and the censoring effect of a 21-game back-injury season are the only reasons he is not lower.
Whoever drafts him in the top 8 is paying full price for pedigree and flair on the thinnest production evidence in the lottery; at 14 the D'Angelo Russell/Klay-shaped upside is finally worth the gamble.
