# 2026 NBA Draft, Team Fit Analysis (Picks 1-30)

Phase 6 output, compiled June 10, 2026. This is the FIT layer, not a talent re-ranking.
Inputs, draft_order.csv, team_context.md, consensus_board.csv (median rank = availability proxy,
a player is treated as plausibly available within roughly +/-6 of his median), comps_2026.csv,
prospect_stats_2026.csv, and film notes for the top 20. No web lookups were needed for this phase,
so no sources_teamfit.md entries were generated. Roster facts come from team_context.md only;
anything that file lacks is marked UNVERIFIED.

## Pick 1: Washington Wizards
**The pick.** Own pick, won the lottery with 14% odds, first No. 1 since John Wall in 2010.
**Needs and timeline.** Rebuilding but vet-accelerated around Alex Sarr, Bilal Coulibaly, Tre Johnson, plus the injured Trae Young and Anthony Davis acquisitions. Needs franchise talent first, then wing shooting and a perimeter defensive identity.
**Best fits plausibly available.**
- AJ Dybantsa (median 1). Elite three-level wing scorer slots cleanly between Sarr's rim protection and Young's table-setting, the wing star this roster lacks.
- Cameron Boozer (median 3). Safest production-and-processing bet in the class, though his frontcourt overlap with Sarr and Davis makes him a slightly awkward 1-1 fit.
- Darryn Peterson (median 2). Elite self-creating shot-maker, but he duplicates Young's and Tre Johnson's on-ball role more than Dybantsa does.
**Fit vs talent.** Talent only at No. 1, and conveniently the consensus No. 1 (Dybantsa) is also the cleanest fit.

## Pick 2: Utah Jazz
**The pick.** Own pick, jumped to No. 2 from a tied-for-worst 22-60 record.
**Needs and timeline.** Rebuilding but trying to turn the corner with Markkanen, the newly acquired Jaren Jackson Jr., Keyonte George and Ace Bailey. Needs a lead initiator and No. 1 perimeter option, point-of-attack defense, and center depth behind Kessler.
**Best fits plausibly available.**
- Darryn Peterson (median 2). The on-ball, all-three-levels shot creator their need list literally starts with, and he fits their teen-upside drafting history.
- Cameron Boozer (median 3). Bully-strength interior scorer who would pair oddly with Markkanen and JJJ positionally but is the best player likely on the board.
- Caleb Wilson (median 4). Explosive two-way forward whose defense helps, though the frontcourt is already the roster's deepest area.
**Fit vs talent.** Take talent, and Peterson is both the consensus No. 2 and the exact archetype they need, so no tension exists.

## Pick 5: LA Clippers (via Indiana)
**The pick.** Acquired from Indiana in the February 2026 Zubac trade (with Mathurin, Isaiah Jackson and a 2029 first), conveyed when the pick landed at exactly 5.
**Needs and timeline.** Aging retool around Kawhi (35 in 2026), Garland and Mathurin, win-now but desperate for a youth pipeline. Needs a starting center, wing athleticism, and long-term star insurance behind Kawhi.
**Best fits plausibly available.**
- Nate Ament (median 10). 6'10 fluid wing with star-scale tools, the one swing on this board that doubles as succession planning behind Kawhi, and his comp cohort (Tatum, Deng, Gay) is exactly that archetype.
- Keaton Wagler (median 5). Consensus best player here, and his off-ball shooting lets him share a backcourt with Garland rather than fight him for the ball.
- Brayden Burries (median 9). Ready-made two-way guard who matches their historical preference for plug-and-play contributors.
- Aday Mara (median 11.5, fringe of the availability window). The only center on the board, 7'3 with elite rim protection, but taking him here stretches the range.
**Fit vs talent.** This is the rare slot where fit legitimately bends the pick, every guard here duplicates Garland, so the wing with the highest ceiling (Ament) is both the fit and the upside play.
