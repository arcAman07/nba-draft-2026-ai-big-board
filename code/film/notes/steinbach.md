# Hannes Steinbach (PF/C, Washington) - Film Notes
Source clip: Washington vs Wisconsin, Big Ten Tournament, 25 pts / 16 reb game (YouTube id kZdN-gVgp5k, 480p, 196 frames at 1 fps)

## Frame-based observations (mine)

- Body frame/build in game context. Steinbach (#6, dark "DAWGS" jersey) reads as a legitimately big, broad-shouldered 5 with a thick lower half; standing among Big Ten frontcourt players he is consistently one of the two tallest bodies on the floor, and his trunk looks filled out rather than stringy. [frame_0184.jpg, frame_0190.jpg, frame_0144.jpg]

- Finishing extension and gather, sequence one. On a left-block gather he keeps the ball high in a two-handed grip at roughly forehead height while a Wisconsin defender walls up, then extends into a high-release finish near the square with the ball above rim level, no dip back down into the crowd. [frame_0144.jpg, frame_0145.jpg, frame_0146.jpg, frame_0147.jpg]

- Finishing extension and second-jump work, sequence two. Around the rim he goes up with full arm extension for a putback/tip attempt with the ball at or above the cylinder, body squared to the backboard, and lands ready to re-engage; in the follow-up frames he is again in a wide rebounding stance under the glass. [frame_0181.jpg, frame_0182.jpg, frame_0183.jpg, frame_0184.jpg]

- Rebounding posture. In multiple rim-area frames he establishes a wide base with knees flexed and both hands up before the ball arrives, claiming space with his hips rather than reaching over the top. [frame_0140.jpg, frame_0146.jpg, frame_0184.jpg]

- Defensive/ready stance depth. Off-ball near the dotted line he sits in a medium-depth stance, feet wider than shoulders, hands out at his sides; it is an upright big's stance rather than a deep guard slide position. [frame_0190.jpg, frame_0184.jpg]

- What these frames cannot support: no clear catch-and-shoot jumper appears in the sampled frames, so I make no claim about his set point or follow-through on perimeter shots, and frames cannot show speed, burst, recovery timing, or feel.

## Sourced scouting observations (human scouts, cited)

- Swish Theory (Matt) calls his rebounding elite, "leading the NCAA in rebounding as a freshman, with 14% oreb and 25% dreb rates," and compares the marks favorably to top freshman big seasons. Source: Swish Theory, https://theswishtheory.com/2026-nba-draft-articles/2026/04/scouting-report-hannes-steinbach/

- Swish Theory also flags interior rim protection as the main defensive concern, noting "a 3.9% block rate at 6'11" is disappointing, casting doubt on his interior defensive value" beyond the glass. Source: Swish Theory, https://theswishtheory.com/2026-nba-draft-articles/2026/04/scouting-report-hannes-steinbach/

- Babcock Hoops praises footwork that is "fundamentally sound and efficient" and "creates scoring angles and maintains balance around the rim," while warning he "lacks lateral quickness; struggles to stay balanced and in front of quicker ball-handlers" and "needs to show more consistency as a perimeter threat to stretch defenses." Source: Babcock Hoops, https://www.babcockhoops.com/post/2026-nba-draft-hannes-steinbach-scouting-report

- SI.com's draft newsfeed describes rebounding as "his best and most elite tool," noting he led college basketball at 11.8 rpg and is "relentless fighting over the opponent's back without fouling." Source: SI.com, https://www.si.com/nba/draft/newsfeed/nba-draft-scouting-report-washington-hannes-steinbach
