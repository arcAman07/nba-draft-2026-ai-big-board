# Cameron Boozer, PF, Duke (2025-26)
Film source, ACC Digital Network midseason highlight reel (YouTube id KwEliYUNeUg), 685 frames at 1 fps. Boozer wears #12; in wide frames where the number is not legible I note that identification rests on this being his player reel.

## Frame-based observations (mine)

- Post-up into rim finish, two-frame rise (frame_0287.jpg through frame_0290.jpg, vs Texas Tech at MSG). He establishes a wide, low post seal on the right block with the defender pinned on his hip (frame_0289), then is airborne at the rim a frame later with the ball carried high and away from the swiping defender (frame_0290). Frames support a strong, wide-base seal and high ball position on the finish; they cannot show how quickly he elevated.
- Second finishing motion, full extension in traffic (frame_0562.jpg, frame_0563.jpg, vs Louisville). In a crowded paint he rises off two feet and reaches full arm extension above the rim line with his torso still vertical rather than drifting. The frames show body control and extension at the catch point of the play, not the takeoff speed.
- Body frame in game context (frame_0289.jpg, frame_0610.jpg). Relative to ACC bigs guarding him, he reads as already filled out, thick through the chest, glutes, and thighs, with a base that holds its width under contact in the post frames. This is consistent across multiple games in the reel.
- Faceup handle posture (frame_0610.jpg, frame_0612.jpg, at Virginia Tech). Isolated on the left wing against a set defender, he holds a bent-knee faceup stance with the ball on his outside hip rather than in front of his body. Frame 0612 shows him having shifted toward the middle with the defender still attached, so the frames support a deliberate, shielded faceup approach but say nothing about separation quickness.
- Off-ball activity context (frame_0020.jpg vs Army, frame_0070.jpg vs Indiana State). He spends sampled possessions in the lane area battling for position rather than spotting far above the arc; in frame_0070 he is mid-lane with arms up as a play finishes. Sampling at 1 fps cannot establish defensive timing or rotation quality, only positioning depth.
- Not claimed from frames, vertical pop, footspeed, screening timing, passing reads; this reel also lacks a clean perimeter jumper rep at the sampled seconds, so no claim on his three-point set point is made.

## Sourced scouting observations (human scouts, cited)

- Babcock Hoops describes Boozer as an elite interior scorer with polished footwork, touch, and body control who "scores through contact, creates angles in the post, and consistently finishes with patience," while cautioning that his shot-creation ceiling is lower than other top prospects and that rim protection is not a strength. Source, Babcock Hoops, https://www.babcockhoops.com/post/2026-nba-draft-cameron-boozer-scouting-report
- HoopsHype's intel notes that at 6'9" and nearly 250 pounds he "plays with force, absorbs contact, and moves defenders off their spots," with an NBA-ready frame and elite rebounding, but that he is not the traditional explosive athlete teams usually take with the first few picks. Source, HoopsHype, https://www.hoopshype.com/story/sports/nba/draft/2026/05/10/cameron-boozer-nba-draft-scouting-report-and-intel/89756181007/
- CBS Sports' comparative scouting piece on Boozer and Caleb Wilson frames the central question around Boozer's athletic profile and how his strength-and-skill game translates, while treating him as one of the safest bets in the class because of his production and processing. Source, CBS Sports, https://www.cbssports.com/nba/news/2026-nba-draft-scouting-cameron-boozer-caleb-wilson/
