## Frame-based observations (mine)

- Pull-up shooting motion vs Kentucky: in the run frame_0120.jpg, frame_0121.jpg, frame_0122.jpg, frame_0123.jpg Brown drives middle and rises into a pull-up. In frame_0122.jpg he is fully elevated with the ball released above his head, shooting arm extended toward the rim and legs nearly straight under him; the trailing defenders are still grounded, and his shoulders stay square to the basket through the rise. The broadcast distance limits judgment of exact set-point height, but the release point is clearly above the forehead.
- Second shooting motion vs SC State: frames frame_0043.jpg, frame_0044.jpg, frame_0045.jpg, frame_0046.jpg show a drive from the wing into an elevated jumper in traffic. In frame_0046.jpg he is airborne in the lane with the ball at the top of the motion over multiple defenders, again with a high, above-the-head release rather than a push shot from the chest.
- A third rise is visible in the Cincinnati sequence (frame_0210.jpg through frame_0214.jpg), where he beats full-court pressure and gets to a wing pull-up by frame_0213.jpg.
- Handle posture under pressure: frame_0210.jpg and frame_0211.jpg show him handling against a Cincinnati double in the backcourt. His posture is more upright than a low-crouch guard, ball kept on the hip away from the trap, head up. The upright carriage recurs at frame_0410.jpg bringing the ball up vs Memphis.
- Body frame/build: across the close enough frames (frame_0210.jpg, frame_0122.jpg, frame_0046.jpg) he reads as a tall, thin guard, long limbs, narrow shoulders, slender legs. Nothing in these frames suggests added bulk; in traffic (frame_0046.jpg) he is noticeably slighter than the bodies around him.
- Frames cannot support claims about burst, vertical pop in real time, decision speed, or passing feel; the wide angles also prevent assessment of base width at the set point or follow-through wrist position.

## Sourced scouting observations (human scouts, cited)

- FanSided/Yahoo Sports scouting report ("Mikel Brown Jr. will electrify and mystify") praises his shooting foundation, "Brown's mechanics are clean, his release consistent, and he has shown he can hit from deep both off the catch and off the dribble," while noting he "relies heavily on tough jumpers" and shot just 34.4% from three. Source, https://fansided.com/nba/nba-draft-scouting-report-mikel-brown-jr-will-electrify-and-mystify (also at https://sports.yahoo.com/articles/nba-draft-scouting-report-mikel-123504483.html)
- Babcock Hoops calls his passing possibly "the single highest passing flashes of the top guard group in the 2026 class, with premier feel in the pick-and-roll," but flags the 3.0+ turnovers per game and a roughly 1.5 assist-to-turnover ratio. Source, https://www.babcockhoops.com/post/2026-nba-draft-mikel-brown-jr-scouting-report
- Pro Football Network's draft outlook notes the lingering back injury that cut his season to 21 games has widened his range, "a threat to go as high as the mid-lottery, but as low as the late-lottery," and lists him around 6-foot-5 with a reported 6-foot-7 wingspan who "lacks the physicality to play through contact." Source, https://www.profootballnetwork.com/nba/mikel-brown-jr-2026-nba-draft-outlook/
