## Verdict

Peterson is the cleanest lead-guard bet in the draft, a freshman who carried 33.5% usage at a positive efficiency with an excellent free-throw clip, low turnovers, and the group's second-best BPM.
The season's only real blemish is the 24-game sample caused by the hamstring, which both shrinks confidence and poisons his statistical comp cohort with low-minute scoring guards he does not actually resemble.
The model still puts him second with a 2.5-to-39.2 VORP band, and the market is literally unanimous at No. 2.
He is third on my board only because Boozer's production and Dybantsa's ceiling sit above him, and at pick 2 Utah gets need and talent in one player.
