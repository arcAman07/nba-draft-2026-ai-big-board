## Market view & board delta

Consensus median 20.0 (min 16, max 27, spread 11); my final rank is 11, a nine-spot rise and the biggest upgrade on my board.
Board rationale: "Major riser: model 6, 0.38 All-Star tier prob (3rd in class), cohort ceiling 24.1 VORP; the market is slow on a Stanford guard whose statistical profile matches lottery guards of the past."
Expanded: the market is anchoring on program (Stanford does not produce lottery guards) and on his 6'1.25" height, while the things the model weighs, age, BPM, free-throw volume and percentage, low turnovers at 31% usage, are all loudly positive.
Half the outlets have him 16, half have him mid-20s; when the disagreement is between scouts' priors and a statistical profile this clean and this young, I side most of the way with the profile, which is why he lands 11 rather than the model's 6 or the market's 20.

## Verdict

Okorie is my biggest swing against the market, and the case is simple: 23.2 points at 31% usage with an 8.7 turnover rate, 7.3 free-throw attempts a game at 83%, all as a 19.2-year-old, is a lottery-guard statistical profile wearing a Stanford jersey.
The model ranks him 6 with the third-best All-Star probability in the class, and his cohort's 24.1 VORP ceiling backs the tail.
The honest risks are size, a slight frame the scouts unanimously flag, and a defensive projection that the 2.2 DBPM does not settle.
Ranking him 11 rather than 6 prices those risks; ranking him 20 like the market would mean ignoring every leading indicator this project was built to catch.
If he is there in the late teens of the real draft, he is the value pick of the night.
