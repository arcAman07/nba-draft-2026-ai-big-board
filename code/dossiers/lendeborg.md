**Age at draft:** 23.73 | **Measurements:** 6'8.75" barefoot (80.75"), 241.4 lbs, 7'3.25" wingspan (87.25"), 9'0.5" standing reach (108.5"), legitimate center dimensions; athletic tests: 10.82s lane agility and 2.80s shuttle (good for his size) against a modest 25.5" standing vertical, 32.0" max vertical, 3.35s sprint.

## Production (2025-26)

- 15.1 ppg, 6.8 rpg, 3.2 apg, 1.1 spg, 1.2 bpg in 30.2 mpg across all 40 games at Michigan; no significant injuries found.
- Efficient everywhere: 51.5 FG%, 37.2 3P% on 4.5 attempts per game, 82.4 FT%, 64.6 TS% on a low-maintenance 20.4% usage.

- The catch is on the birth certificate: he turns 24 in September, so all of this came as a fifth-year player against younger competition.
- Forty games at full health rounds out the profile: durability, efficiency and two-way impact with no single statistical weakness to point at.

## Verdict

Lendeborg is the most certain rotation player in this stretch of the board and the least likely star, and every information source agrees on both halves.
A 16.7 BPM season at 64.6 TS% with a 7'3.25" wingspan and five-position defensive credibility is a finished product an NBA coach can use in October.
The 0% cohort All-Star rate and the model's age discount are the same fact stated twice: 23.73-year-old rookies do not become stars, they become role players or they wash out.
At 12 I am happy to pay for the Cam Johnson/Bridges version of that trade, and the 13.3% bust rate says the downside is thinner than for almost any upside swing available in the same range.
