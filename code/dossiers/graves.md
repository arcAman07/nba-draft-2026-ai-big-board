# 15. Allen Graves — PF, Santa Clara (Tier 3)

**Age at draft:** 19.91 | **Measurements:** 6'7.75" barefoot (79.75"), 225.6 lbs, 7'0" wingspan (84.0"), 8'10.5" standing reach (106.5"); athletic testing was the weak spot: 27.5" standing vertical, 34.0" max vertical, 11.76s lane agility, 3.18s shuttle and 3.44s sprint, all toward the bottom of the combine field and consistent with the mobility concerns scouts raise.

## Production (2025-26)

- 11.8 ppg, 6.5 rpg, 1.8 apg, 1.9 spg, 0.9 bpg in just 22.6 mpg across 35 games, largely as Santa Clara's sixth man in the WCC; no significant injuries found.
- Shooting and security: 51.2 FG%, 41.3 3P% on 2.6 attempts per game, 75.0 FT%, 61.3 TS% on 21.9% usage with a tiny 6.9 TOV%.
- The defensive event creation is the outlier trait: 1.9 steals plus 0.9 blocks in 22.6 minutes from a forward.
- Advanced: 13.4 BPM (8.7 OBPM, 4.7 DBPM) at age 19.9, but earned against WCC competition, which matters below.
- Per-minute, the line scales to a much louder season than the raw 11.8 points suggests; the bench role, not the impact, kept the counting stats modest.

## Model view

- Predicted VORP: median 3.9, with a p25-p75 band of 1.5 to 19.7 (mean 5.8, model rank 5).
- Tier probabilities (top 2): Rotation 0.42, Starter 0.38. Bust probability 0.03, the lowest of my eight prospects.
- Flags: none, which is itself the problem: the model has no conference-strength adjustment (a documented limitation), so a 13.4 BPM in the WCC reads the same as one in the Big 12.
- The drivers are age 19.9 plus an enormous college BPM with strong TS% and steal/block rates; the model is essentially betting the production is real wherever it happened.

## Market view & board delta

Consensus median 20.0 (min 17, max 28, spread 11); my final rank is 15, a five-spot rise on the market and ten below the model's 5.
Board rationale: "Model 5 (13.4 BPM at 19.9) with J-Richardson/Otto Porter comps, but WCC competition has no conference-strength adjustment in the model (documented limitation); split the difference between model 5 and consensus 20."
Expanded: this riser case is a measurement problem, not a disagreement about the player. The model sees a teenager with a top-five statistical season and prices him like a top-five pick; the market sees a WCC sixth man and prices him in the 20s, and both are responding rationally to the inputs they trust.
Because the model's blindness to conference strength is documented in our own iteration notes, I cannot take rank 5 at face value, but a 41% shooting forward with rare defensive instincts at age 19.9 is not a 20th pick either; 15 splits the difference deliberately.

- Probability read: a one-in-five bust rate is the highest among my eight, but the 4.4 median and 23.4 ceiling say the middle and top of the distribution are genuinely valuable, a Porter/Miller-grade shooting forward.
- Net shape: lower floor than his low model bust probability suggests, real starter median, and a tail worth holding at non-lottery cost.

- Wing catch into a lane attack vs Saint Mary's (frame_0497 through frame_0499): shot-ready catch posture with knees loaded, then a rise in the lane through contact with the ball protected high above the contest; the stills say nothing about how quickly he beat the closeout.
- Defensive stance depth vs San Diego (frame_1401 through frame_1404): a wide, low point-of-attack stance with hips dropped that funnels the drive into help where the ball comes loose; the frames support genuinely deep stance posture but cannot confirm lateral speed, or even that this defender is Graves specifically at this resolution.
- Explicit shooting caveat, preserved: across the 33 sampled frames there was no clean, unambiguous jump-shot rep (set point, base, follow-through), since the reel's cuts resolve at the rim or fall between 1 fps samples, so no frame-based claim is made about his jumper mechanics despite shooting being his headline scouted strength. Mobility, bounce, closing speed and the timing on his steals and blocks are likewise not claimable from stills.

### Scouting consensus (cited)

- Sports Business Classroom: about 22.6 minutes as the sixth man producing 11.8/6.5/1.9 steals on 51% from the floor and 41% from three, with the most reliable offensive translation being shooting plus offensive rebounding.
- Babcock Hoops: "truly remarkable defensive playmaking instincts" for his size, roughly a 5.3 steal percentage and 5.3 block percentage, an extremely rare freshman-forward combination, while cautioning he is not super mobile or bouncy, part of why he finished only about 52% of his layups.
- At The Hive (Charlotte's team blog): a 6'9", roughly 225-pound forward with intriguing all-around value, a three-level scorer with soft touch, good passing feel and encouraging shooting upside.

## Team fits

- He appears in the fit notes for six different picks from 18 through 23, the broadest late-first demand of any forward on the board.

## Verdict

Graves is the board's quietest riser and the cleanest test of how much to trust our own model: rank 5 by the numbers, rank 20 by the market, with a documented model blind spot (no conference-strength adjustment) sitting exactly between them.
What survives any WCC discount is the trait combination, 41% three-point shooting and a steal-plus-block rate that is nearly unique for a freshman forward, attached to a 19.9-year-old with a 3% modeled bust probability.
What does not survive is the athletic testing, which was poor across the board and matches the scouts' "not super mobile or bouncy" warning, and the cohort's 20% bust rate keeps the floor honest.
At 15 he is priced as a strong starter bet with a Jason Richardson-flavored tail, and if he is still on the board in the 20s of the real draft he is plain value.
