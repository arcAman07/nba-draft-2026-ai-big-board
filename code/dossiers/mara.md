# 10. Aday Mara — C, Michigan (Tier 2)

**Age at draft:** 21.22 | **Measurements:** 7'3" barefoot (87.0"), 259.8 lbs, 7'6" wingspan (90.0"), 9'9" standing reach (117.0", historic for the combine); athletic testing was the weak side of the card: 24.0" standing vertical, 28.0" max vertical, 11.47s lane agility, 3.36s shuttle, 3.61s sprint, all near the bottom of the class.

## Production (2025-26)

- 12.1 ppg, 6.8 rpg, 2.4 apg, 2.6 bpg, 0.4 spg in only 23.4 mpg across a full 40-game Michigan season; no significant injuries found.
- Outrageous interior efficiency: 66.8 FG%, 65.9 TS% on 23.1% usage, almost no perimeter volume (0.3 threes attempted per game) and a real wart at the line, 56.4 FT% on 3.5 FTA.
- Advanced: 14.3 BPM with a defense-tilted split (5.8 OBPM, 8.4 DBPM), and a genuinely unusual 19.0 AST% for a center against a high 17.7 TOV%.
- The per-minute production is elite; the questions are foot speed, free throws and whether 23 minutes a night is a feature or a limit.
- The 2.6 blocks against 0.4 steals is the profile in miniature: he erases shots at the rim and creates almost nothing in passing lanes or space.

## Model view

- Predicted VORP: median 3.9, with a p25-p75 band of 1.1 to 22.1 (mean 4.7, model rank 9).
- Tier probabilities (top 2): Bench 0.51, Starter 0.20. Bust probability 0.07.
- Flags: none.
- The model's love is driven by his huge college BPM, elite TS% and class-leading block production, while the 56.4 FT% and age 21.2 feed the bimodal shape: a 0.51 Bench probability sitting under a fat starter-or-better tail.

## Historical comps & outcomes

- Ceiling comp: Evan Mobley (12.1 career VORP). Floor comp: Hasheem Thabeet (-0.4); Roy Hibbert (3.5), Brendan Haywood (2.9) and Udoka Azubuike (0.1) round out the list.
- Cohort of 15: floor 0.5 VORP, median 2.8, ceiling 17.4; bust rate 6.7%, All-Star rate 26.7%. Confidence HIGH.
- Mismatch notes are extensive and cut both ways: Hibbert is a rim-protection mismatch (his 12.0 BLK% vs the comp's 6.3), Mobley and Azubuike are size mismatches (87" vs 83"/82"), Thabeet is a creation-role mismatch (Mara's 19.0 AST% vs 3.0).
- Probability read: roughly a 1-in-4 historical All-Star rate and a tiny bust rate, but the median outcome is a solid backup-grade center; the passing is the trait none of the cautionary comps had.

### Scouting consensus (cited)

- Babcock Hoops (Matt Babcock): rim protection is "tremendous," a natural shot-blocker with excellent timing and verticality who rarely needs to leave the ground, but he "lacks quickness in space" and can be exposed in pick-and-roll and transition.
- Floor and Ceiling (Wilko Martínez-Cachero): a "really creative passer for a center" who throws no-looks and over-the-heads from the post and elbow, while flagging the "awful" 46.5 FT% (40/86) and easy points left on the table.
- Babcock Hoops also notes soft touch and developing confidence on mid-range and pick-and-pop attempts, hinting at stretch potential despite the interior role.

## Verdict

Mara is the board's most legible bet: a 9'9" standing reach, 14.3 BPM and 2.6 blocks a game say the rim-protection floor is real, and the 19% assist rate separates him from every cautionary tale in his cohort.
The combine testing and the 56.4 FT% are exactly why the model parks half his probability mass at Bench, and that scenario, a Thabeet-path backup who gets played off the floor in May, is genuinely live.
But the cohort carries a 26.7% All-Star rate against a 6.7% bust rate, and at pick 10-12 cost that asymmetry is priced correctly.
Take him at 10, accept that the median outcome is a very good backup, and treat anything Mobley-shaped as the free tail.
