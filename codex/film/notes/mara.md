# Aday Mara (C, Michigan) - Film Notes, 2026 NBA Draft
Source clip, Final Four vs Arizona (26 pts), YouTube id 6F5pUJib3Y0, 248 frames at 1 fps.

## Frame-based observations (mine)
- High two-hand gather and overhead release near the rim. Mara secures the ball at full extension above the defense and releases close to backboard height with his right hand, elbow nearly locked out at release. Finishing extension is vertical rather than a sweeping hook. (frame_0059.jpg, frame_0060.jpg)
- Standing reach context. Standing flat-footed under the basket his head sits roughly at the bottom edge of the backboard, so set point on any close shot starts above typical contest height before he leaves the floor. (frame_0061.jpg)
- Body frame in game context. Close-up frames show a lean upper body for 7'3", relatively slim shoulders and arms with visible room to add mass through the chest and trunk. Lower body looks proportionally longer than his torso. (frame_0196.jpg, frame_0220.jpg)
- Handle and post posture. When he catches at the elbow or mid-post he keeps the ball high at chest-to-chin level with two hands, torso upright, and shields with his off shoulder rather than lowering into a deep dribble crouch. (frame_0091.jpg, frame_0181.jpg, frame_0182.jpg)
- Contested finishing through traffic. Overhead-camera frame shows a two-hand gather directly at the rim with a defender on his body, ball kept above shoulder line throughout the visible sequence. (frame_0178.jpg, frame_0179.jpg, frame_0180.jpg, frame_0181.jpg)
- Rim positioning on offense. In half-court frames he is repeatedly stationed inside or at the edge of the lane near the dotted circle rather than spaced beyond the arc, consistent with an interior-first role in this game. (frame_0030.jpg, frame_0115.jpg, frame_0116.jpg)
- Frames cannot support claims about his speed, recovery quickness, block timing, or decision speed, so none are made here.

## Sourced scouting observations (human scouts, cited)
- Matt Babcock, Babcock Hoops: Mara's "rim protection is tremendous"; he is a "natural shot-blocker with excellent timing and verticality; rarely needs to leave the ground to affect attempts," though he "lacks quickness in space; can be exposed in pick-and-roll coverages and transition." https://www.babcockhoops.com/post/2026-nba-draft-aday-mara-scouting-report
- Wilko Martínez-Cachero, Floor and Ceiling (Substack): "really creative passer for a center" who "throws no-looks, behind-the-backs, over-the-heads" from the post and elbow; flags that his "46.5 FT% is awful (40/86)" and that "he leaves a ton of easy points on the table" given his high free throw rate. https://floorandceiling.substack.com/p/aday-mara-scouting-report
- Babcock Hoops report also notes "soft touch and developing confidence on mid-range jumpers and pick-and-pop attempts," suggesting stretch potential despite the interior role. https://www.babcockhoops.com/post/2026-nba-draft-aday-mara-scouting-report
