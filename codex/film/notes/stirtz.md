## Frame-based observations (mine)

- Handle posture. On a live drive against an Ohio State defender he keeps the ball on a low right-hand dribble below knee height with his chest forward over the ball, shoulders level, and eyes up rather than down at the floor; the off-hand is up as a guard arm. [frame_0095.jpg, frame_0020.jpg]

- Shooting motion, sequence one (jumper, first half). Across consecutive frames he moves from a dribble into an elevated release near the free-throw-line-extended area, finishing at the top of his jump with both arms extended fully overhead and the shooting arm straight on the follow-through; teammates and the bench are already rising in the next frame. The visible release point is high relative to his head, not a set shot from the chest. [frame_0043.jpg, frame_0044.jpg, frame_0045.jpg, frame_0046.jpg]

- Shooting motion, sequence two (drive-and-finish, first half). He attacks from the right side with a low dribble, gets into the lane, and rises off two feet with the ball secured at head height before extending toward the rim; the gather is compact, the ball is not exposed below his waist in traffic. [frame_0034.jpg, frame_0035.jpg, frame_0036.jpg, frame_0033.jpg]

- Body frame/build in game context. In close-up he reads as a lean, narrow-shouldered guard with long legs and a slim trunk next to bigger Ohio State bodies; there is visible but not heavy muscle in the arms and shoulders. [frame_0096.jpg, frame_0097.jpg, frame_0095.jpg]

- What these frames cannot support: 1 fps frames cannot establish his burst, separation quickness, lateral defensive speed, or decision timing, and no clean on-ball defensive stance close-up appeared in my sample, so I make no frame claim about his stance depth.

## Sourced scouting observations (human scouts, cited)

- Yahoo Sports' draft scouting report praises the shooting and feel, noting he "hit a ridiculous 51.7 percent from 3 on his catch-and-shoot attempts this season" and calling him one of the more prolific pick-and-roll guards to pass through the draft ranks in some time. Source: Yahoo Sports, https://sports.yahoo.com/articles/2026-nba-draft-scouting-report-110000082.html

- Peachtree Hoops (SB Nation) flags the athleticism question, writing that lottery-level guards "all have some sort of speed, strength, or twitch" and that Stirtz "just doesn't have that extra burst, which could limit him on offense if you're asking him to go get a bucket in isolation." Source: Peachtree Hoops, https://www.peachtreehoops.com/atlanta-hawks-nba-draft/71457/2026-nba-draft-scouting-report-bennett-stirtz

- NBC Sports Philadelphia's profile calls him "skilled, versatile, and polished" with arguably the best feel in the class, while noting his point-of-attack defense at Iowa "hasn't been great," that he "doesn't move his feet laterally particularly well," and can get overpowered by stronger players. Source: NBC Sports Philadelphia, https://www.nbcsportsphiladelphia.com/nba/philadelphia-76ers/sixers-draft-profile-bennett-stirtz-iowa-basketball-nba-draft/735360/
