# Darryn Peterson, SG, Kansas (2025-26)
Film source, single-game highlight reel, 27 pts vs Kansas State, March 7 2026, Allen Fieldhouse (YouTube id jlMx6ZcAgho), 210 frames at 1 fps. Kansas in crimson alternates, Peterson is #22.

## Frame-based observations (mine)

- Pull-up sequence, drive to elevated release (frame_0057.jpg through frame_0061.jpg). He attacks right against K-State's #10 with a low, forward-leaning drive posture (frame_0057, frame_0058), shields the ball on the gather with his shoulders turned into the defender (frame_0059), then rises into a pull-up with the ball above the rim line of the contesting big #15 (frame_0060), ball dropping through at frame_0061. The frames support a high release over a longer contester and a balanced vertical body line at the apex; they cannot show how quickly he got from the gather to the release.
- Second shooting motion, held follow-through (frame_0120.jpg, frame_0121.jpg). On a midrange jumper from the right elbow area, his shooting arm is still fully extended with a relaxed wrist a beat after the ball has left, elbow above eye level, suggesting a repeatable high-finish follow-through on this attempt. One-second sampling means the dip and set point of this rep are not captured.
- Handle posture in transition (frame_0089.jpg, frame_0088.jpg). Pushing the ball upcourt he keeps a deep knee bend with the ball dribbled outside his frame on the left side, chest up and eyes ahead. Frames show posture and ball placement, not speed.
- Body frame in game context (frame_0058.jpg, frame_0059.jpg). Side-on drive frames show a filled-out guard build, thick through the shoulders and thighs relative to the K-State guards in frame, with long arms that let him hold the ball away from the defender's reach on the gather.
- Finishing/decision context (frame_0091.jpg). Attacking the left block he is gathering off two feet in traffic with the ball held high and away from the help defender, torso square to the backboard. Extension at the rim itself falls between sampled frames.
- Not claimed from frames, burst, separation speed, defensive timing, passing feel; the reel is also one game, so these are single-game snapshots.

## Sourced scouting observations (human scouts, cited)

- Babcock Hoops calls Peterson an elite shot creator with "one of the deepest scoring bags in the class," scoring off pull-ups, step-backs, movement shooting, and tough self-created jumpers, while warning his shot selection "can swing game to game" with nights he settles for difficult jumpers. Source, Babcock Hoops, https://www.babcockhoops.com/post/2026-nba-draft-darryn-peterson-scouting-report
- HoopsHype's draft intel projects him as an elite isolation scorer and high-level pull-up shot-maker because of his balance, pace, and ability to rise into jumpers from all three levels, though scouts noted a season complicated by missed time and rhythm questions. Source, HoopsHype, https://www.hoopshype.com/story/sports/nba/draft/2026/05/10/darryn-peterson-nba-draft-scouting-report-and-intel/89532988007/
- Sports Illustrated's draft newsfeed highlights his blend of positional size at 6'6" with a strong frame and long arms, scoring versatility, and rare-for-his-age basketball IQ, giving him legitimate star potential near the top of the draft. Source, SI.com, https://www.si.com/nba/draft/newsfeed/nba-draft-scouting-report-kansas-guard-darryn-peterson
