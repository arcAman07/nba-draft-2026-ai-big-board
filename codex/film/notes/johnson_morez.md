Source clip: "Morez Johnson Jr. 2026 NCAA tournament highlights" (YouTube id agQhxCcfGSc, 480p, 457 frames at 1 fps). Note, the clip is a multi-game tournament reel (Howard, Saint Louis, Alabama, Tennessee, Arizona, UConn), so I only attribute frames where the featured player is clearly the subject of the play or shown in close-up.

## Frame-based observations (mine)

- Body frame/build in game context. The close-up frame shows a genuinely wide, muscled shoulder girdle and thick arms with twists hair; in game frames the same build reads as a power big whose torso is noticeably denser than the wings around him. [frame_0035.jpg, frame_0012.jpg]

- Finishing extension, sequence one (vs Howard). The featured player drives the lane, gathers the ball low with two hands inside the dotted circle, then finishes above the rim with the ball arm fully extended over the front of the cylinder, hips squared to the backboard, off-arm tucked rather than flailing. [frame_0011.jpg, frame_0012.jpg, frame_0013.jpg, frame_0014.jpg]

- Finishing extension, sequence two (Final Four vs Arizona). On a baseline attack the dark-jersey Michigan big elevates with the ball brought up high above shoulder level into a one-hand extension at the rim through a contesting defender, finishing with the elbow nearly locked out. [frame_0337.jpg, frame_0338.jpg, frame_0339.jpg]

- Rim pursuit posture. In the lane gather frame his body is already loaded with knees bent and chest over the ball before liftoff, a compact two-hand secure rather than an exposed one-hand scoop. [frame_0013.jpg]

- What these frames cannot support: the sampled frames contain no clear jump-shot or free-throw release from him, so I make no claim about his set point, release height, or follow-through on shots away from the rim. Frames also cannot show his lateral speed, second-jump quickness, or defensive timing, and since this is a multi-game team reel several wide frames (e.g. frame_0060.jpg, frame_0160.jpg, frame_0210.jpg) could not be confidently attributed to him at all, so I excluded them from player-specific claims.

## Sourced scouting observations (human scouts, cited)

- Floor & Ceiling's scouting report argues Johnson is "one of the best defenders in the entire class, better on that end than pretty much every consensus lottery pick," noting he can hold his ground on the block while moving laterally to defend pick-and-roll actions. Source: Floor & Ceiling (Substack), https://floorandceiling.substack.com/p/morez-johnson-jr-scouting-report

- NBC Sports Philadelphia's draft profile highlights his combine athleticism, with lane agility, three-quarter-court sprint, and max vertical "all among the best for big men," at a measured 6'9" barefoot with a 7'3.5" wingspan. Source: NBC Sports Philadelphia, https://www.nbcsportsphiladelphia.com/nba/philadelphia-76ers/sixers-draft-profile-morez-johnson-jr-nba-draft-2026-michigan-basketball/734631/

- Babcock Hoops and SI's report flag the offensive limitations, describing a game that "can be raw at times, particularly when asked to operate outside of structured situations," with no consistent pull-up or mid-range package and only 35 three-point attempts (34.3%) all season, making spot-up development the key swing skill. Sources: Babcock Hoops, https://www.babcockhoops.com/post/2026-nba-draft-morez-johnson-jr-scouting-report and SI.com, https://www.si.com/nba/draft/newsfeed/nba-draft-scouting-report-michigan-forward-morez-johnson-jr
