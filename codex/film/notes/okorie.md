# Ebuka Okorie (PG, Stanford) - Film Notes
Source clip: "Ebuka Okorie 2025-26 Season Highlights | Stanford Men's Basketball" (YouTube id L_q1QA43VFA, 480p, 302 frames at 1 fps). Okorie is Stanford #1, confirmed by the name and number on his jersey in close-up and an on-screen stat graphic ("1 EBUKA OKORIE").

## Frame-based observations (mine)

- Shooting motion, sequence one (game-winner at Virginia Tech). Across consecutive frames he rises into a pull-up from the left wing with the ball brought up in front of his face into a set point above his head, then a full one-arm follow-through with the wrist snapped while still airborne; his landing base looks balanced, feet under him, and the very next frames show the bench erupting. Release height is high for his size because the ball starts above the forehead, not at the chest. [frame_0004.jpg, frame_0005.jpg, frame_0006.jpg, frame_0007.jpg, frame_0010.jpg]

- Shooting motion, sequence two (vs Louisville at home). On a right-side catch he gets the ball overhead quickly into a compact, no-dip release; in the following frames the possession continues with him relocating, and the elevation again appears to come off a narrow but square base. [frame_0160.jpg, frame_0161.jpg, frame_0162.jpg, frame_0163.jpg]

- Body frame/build in game context. Close-ups show a lean, wiry guard with long arms relative to his height, slim waist and lighter upper body next to college wings; the frame is visibly slighter than the forwards around him in walk-up shots. [frame_0010.jpg, frame_0220.jpg, frame_0255.jpg]

- Handle posture. In half-court frames he plays with knees bent and the ball kept on a low outside dribble, torso upright with eyes scanning, rather than bent over the ball. [frame_0080.jpg, frame_0150.jpg]

- What these frames cannot support: 1 fps stills cannot show his celebrated change of pace, burst in short spaces, or defensive hand activity, and I found no clear isolated on-ball defensive stance close-up in the sample, so stance depth is not assessed from frames.

## Sourced scouting observations (human scouts, cited)

- Yahoo Sports' scouting report notes he is "not a freak athlete in the traditional sense, with no elite vertical pop," yet is "a jitterbug with elite change of pace and burst in short spaces," and highlights a usage rate above 31 percent with one of the lowest turnover rates among high-usage freshmen in the modern era. Source: Yahoo Sports, https://sports.yahoo.com/articles/2026-nba-draft-scouting-report-120000147.html

- The combine measurements scouts cite are 6'1.25" barefoot with a 6'7.75" wingspan at about 186 pounds, with reports (SI, Yahoo) agreeing the primary development need is "usable core strength" and continued physicality against NBA competition. Sources: SI.com, https://www.si.com/nba/draft/newsfeed/nba-draft-scouting-report-ebuka-okorie and Yahoo Sports, https://sports.yahoo.com/articles/2026-nba-draft-scouting-report-120000147.html

- Peachtree Hoops' breakdown describes him as "pesky" on the ball with quick hands and active feet, using his wingspan to poke away steals (1.6 per game) and disrupt passing lanes, and projects him as an attacking downhill point guard with a clear offensive identity. Source: Peachtree Hoops, https://www.peachtreehoops.com/atlanta-hawks-game-threads/71067/ebuka-okorie-nba-draft-prospect-atlanta-hawks-video-stats-breakdown-scouting-report
