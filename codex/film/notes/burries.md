## Sourced scouting observations (human scouts, cited)

- Sports Illustrated's draft newsfeed calls Burries a strong, competitive two-way guard around 6'4" with a slightly plus wingspan who brings scoring from all three levels, using physicality, body control, and footwork to create efficient looks near the hoop and in the mid-range. Source, SI.com, https://www.si.com/nba/draft/newsfeed/nba-draft-scouting-report-arizona-guard-brayden-burries
- Babcock Hoops notes his defensive profile could translate immediately, citing a strong frame, toughness, and the lateral mobility to guard multiple perimeter positions, with quick hands and good anticipation generating turnovers, while flagging mid-tier athleticism and 6'4" combo-guard size as the main detractor arguments. Source, Babcock Hoops, https://www.babcockhoops.com/post/2026-nba-draft-brayden-burries-scouting-report
- Yahoo Sports' mock-draft coverage projects Burries as a first-round pick, with Bleacher Report's Jonathan Wasserman slotting him to the Chicago Bulls at No. 9, after he finished as one of the most impactful freshmen in the country for a Final Four Arizona team. Source, Yahoo Sports, https://sports.yahoo.com/articles/brayden-burries-nba-mock-draft-231334190.html
