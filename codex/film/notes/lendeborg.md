# Yaxel Lendeborg (PF, Michigan) - Film Notes, 2026 NBA Draft
Source clips, NCAA second round vs Saint Louis game recap (YouTube id cjAekSOKNL8, frames frame_0001-0640, game action mostly frame_0001-0035 plus interview close-ups) and 2025-26 season highlight reel (YouTube id 1ZjXxA0dgng, frames hl_0001-hl_0203).

## Frame-based observations (mine)
- Mature, NBA-ready build in game context. Postgame close-ups show broad shoulders, thick arms, and a filled-out chest and trunk for a college forward; nothing about the frame reads underdeveloped. (frame_0016.jpg, frame_0060.jpg, frame_0070.jpg)
- Two-hand overhead finish under contact. In the Saint Louis clip he elevates with the ball secured in both hands above his head while a defender (#9) wraps him, keeping the ball high through the foul rather than bringing it down. (frame_0019.jpg, frame_0021.jpg, frame_0022.jpg)
- Lane elevation on a one-footed gather. In the season reel he rises in the paint with the ball raised to the right side of his head, shoulders square to the rim, off-arm up for protection. (hl_0148.jpg, hl_0149.jpg, hl_0150.jpg)
- Defensive/ready posture. Around a loose-ball scramble he plays in a wide base with bent knees and hands active rather than standing upright, holding balance while traffic falls around him. (frame_0080.jpg)
- Perimeter comfort for his size. Season-reel frames show him stationed outside the arc and operating on the wing in Michigan's offense rather than parked exclusively in the dunker spot. (hl_0050.jpg, hl_0052.jpg, hl_0147.jpg)
- Transition involvement. Multiple frames catch him filling lanes in the open floor with Michigan's break across different games. (hl_0030.jpg, hl_0031.jpg, hl_0190.jpg)
- Frames cannot support claims about his first-step burst, lateral recovery speed, or decision timing, so none are made here.

## Sourced scouting observations (human scouts, cited)
- Jonathan Givony, ESPN/DraftExpress: Lendeborg "measured 6'8.75 barefoot and 241 pounds at the NBA Draft Combine, with a 7'3.25 wingspan and 9'0.5 standing reach... outstanding dimensions for a player who slid seamlessly from the 3 to the 5." https://x.com/DraftExpress/status/2053934098117546286
- ESPN big board (Givony/Woo): offers "an extremely rare level of versatility" with "legitimate center-sized dimensions who is comfortable on the perimeter and can guard all five positions in a pinch," projected in the back half of the lottery, though his age suggests "a high-end role player at best." https://www.espn.com/nba/story/_/id/46886245/2026-nba-draft-big-board-rankings-top-100-prospects-players
- Yahoo Sports mock draft scouting report: a "Swiss Army knife" forward whose "vision from the high post and short roll stands out"; his three-point shot "was a bit shaky" at UAB but at Michigan "he's hit a solid 36 percent... he's not a lights-out shooter, but he's also not the type of player that you would willingly leave open." https://sports.yahoo.com/articles/yaxel-lendeborg-nba-mock-draft-112001558.html
